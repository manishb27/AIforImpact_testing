Here are some low-cost agri-products packaging options:

1. **Biodegradable Packaging**: Made from natural materials like cornstarch, sugarcane, or potato starch, biodegradable packaging is a sustainable and eco-friendly option. It's compostable and can reduce plastic waste.

Cost: $0.50-$1.50 per unit

2. **Recycled Paper Packaging**: Using recycled paper products like cardboard, paper pulp, or paper bags can reduce waste and minimize environmental impact.

Cost: $0.25-$0.75 per unit

3. **Compostable Bags**: Made from plant-based materials like PLA (polylactic acid) or PBAT (polybutylene adipate-co-butylene terephthalate), compostable bags are biodegradable and can be composted at home.

Cost: $0.50-$1.50 per unit

4. **Glass Packaging**: Glass is a sustainable and non-toxic material that can be reused and recycled. It's ideal for packaging products like jams, honey, and spices.

Cost: $1.00-$3.00 per unit

5. **Molded Pulp Packaging**: Made from recycled paper products and molded into various shapes, molded pulp packaging is a cost-effective and eco-friendly option for packaging fruits, vegetables, and other food items.

Cost: $0.25-$0.75 per unit

6. **Bamboo Packaging**: Bamboo is a sustainable and renewable resource that can be used to create packaging materials like boxes, bags, and containers.

Cost: $0.50-$1.50 per unit

7. **Reusable Containers**: Using reusable containers made from materials like stainless steel, glass, or silicone can reduce packaging waste and minimize environmental impact.

Cost: $1.00-$5.00 per unit

8. **Edible Packaging**: Edible packaging made from natural ingredients like seaweed, algae, or fruit pulp can reduce plastic waste and provide a sustainable alternative.

Cost: $0.50-$1.50 per unit

9. **Compostable Films**: Compostable films made from plant-based materials like PLA or PBAT can be used to package food items like meat, dairy products, and baked goods.

Cost: $0.50-$1.50 per unit

10. **Local and Regional Packaging**: Partnering with local suppliers to source packaging materials can reduce transportation costs and minimize environmental impact.

Cost: Varies depending on location and supplier

When choosing a low-cost agri-products packaging option, consider the following factors:

* Material cost
* Production cost
* Transportation cost
* Environmental impact
* Customer acceptance
* Compliance with regulations and standards

Remember to always prioritize the safety and quality of your products when selecting packaging materials.
